It is not.Imagine you give your friend a textbook and ask them a question. A smart friend does not re-read the entire book every time. They flip to the relevant chapter, read just that bit, and answer you. That is exactly what RAG does:You upload a document. LangChain breaks it into small chunks (paragraphs).ChromaDB stores each chunk as a coordinate in a special space (called a vector).You ask a question. ChromaDB finds the chunks whose coordinates are closest to your question.Those chunks plus your question are sent to OpenAI.OpenAI reads only those relevant chunks and gives you a precise, cited answer.This is why the answers are accurate and come with citations â€” the AI only answers from actual text in your document, not from guesswork.9.
